Chicken Tikka Masala Recipe

Description

This dish is made with chicken that gets marinated in a spicy curry sauce with plain yogurt and cooked with tomatoes, spices, and more plain yogurt. You can use breasts or thighs, and it's great served with rice.

Details

Author: Lisa Lin

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Total Time: 35 minutes

Cuisine: Indian

Course: Main Course

Servings: 4 to 6 servings

Calories: 324 kcal

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 1 1/4 pounds boneless skinless chicken breasts, thighs, or a mix
  • 6 tablespoons plain whole milk yogurt
  • 1/2 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 1/4 teaspoons salt

For the Tikka Masala Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons canola oil, divided
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced (about 5 ounces, or 1 1/2 cups sliced)
  • 2 teaspoons grated ginger
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 (14-ounce) can crushed fire-roasted tomatoes (or regular crushed tomatoes)
  • 6 tablespoons plain whole milk yogurt
  • 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

To Serve:

  • Cooked rice
  • Cilantro, to garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the chicken: Trim chicken thighs of excess fat and chop into bite-sized pieces. Combine chicken with yogurt, ginger, garlic, cumin, paprika, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Marinate: Cover and refrigerate for at least 45 minutes or up to overnight (4–6 hours is ideal).
  3. Cook the chicken: Heat 1 tablespoon of canola oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the chicken for 6–7 minutes until done. Set aside.
  4. Toast the spices: Wipe down the pan, heat remaining canola oil over medium heat, and cook onions for 5 minutes. Add ginger, garlic, coriander, paprika, garam masala, turmeric, black pepper, salt, and cayenne. Stir until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  5. Make the sauce: Add crushed tomatoes to the spices and cook for 4 minutes, stirring. Add yogurt and combine.
  6. Simmer: Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer the sauce for 4 minutes. Add chicken to the sauce and coat well.
  7. Serve: Serve over cooked basmati rice and garnish with cilantro.

Notes

  • Leftover chicken tikka masala tastes phenomenal the next day because the spices develop more flavor overnight.
  • Avoid using Greek yogurt if possible. If using Greek yogurt, mix it with whole milk to get the consistency of regular yogurt for a sauce that doesn’t separate.
