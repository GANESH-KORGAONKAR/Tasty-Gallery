Butter Chicken Recipe

Description

This is a Chef recipe and one of the easiest Indian curries to make. The Butter Chicken Sauce is so good that you will want it on tap! Many restaurants add copious amounts of ghee or butter into the sauce, but this version is rich enough as it is. Try the streamlined One-Pan Baked Butter Chicken next time!

Details

Author: Nagi | RecipeTin Eats

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Marinating Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 25 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour 5 minutes

Cuisine: Indian

Course: Main Course

Servings: 3–4

Calories: 402 kcal

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 1/2 cup plain yoghurt, full fat
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp chilli powder or cayenne pepper powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp freshly grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1.5 lb / 750 g chicken thigh fillets, cut into bite-size pieces

Curry

  • 2 tbsp (30 g) ghee or butter, OR 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 cup tomato passata (aka tomato puree)
  • 1 cup heavy/thickened cream
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 1/4 tsp salt

To Serve

  • Basmati rice
  • White rice
  • Coriander/cilantro (optional)

Instructions

  1. Optional blitz: For an extra smooth sauce, combine the Marinade ingredients (except the chicken) in a food processor and blend until smooth.
  2. Marinade: Combine the Marinade ingredients with the chicken in a bowl. Cover and refrigerate overnight or up to 24 hours (minimum 3 hours).
  3. Cook chicken: Heat the ghee (or butter/oil) over high heat in a large fry pan. Take the chicken out of the Marinade but do not shake off excess. Cook for around 3 minutes or until the chicken is white all over.
  4. Sauce: Add tomato passata, cream, sugar, and salt to the pan, along with any remaining marinade. Simmer on low heat for 20 minutes. Adjust salt to taste.
  5. Garnish with coriander/cilantro leaves if using. Serve with basmati rice.

Notes

  • Use pure chilli powder, not American-style chili powder blends.
  • Ghee is clarified butter but can be substituted with regular butter or neutral oil.
  • Tomato passata can be substituted with pureed canned tomatoes.
  • For a lighter version, use 3/4 cup light cream + 1/4 cup milk.
  • Quick papadums: Microwave raw papadums for 45–60 seconds until puffed.
