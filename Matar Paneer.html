Matar Paneer (Indian Cottage Cheese & Green Peas)

Description

This homemade Matar Paneer is a family recipe spiced to perfection. Made with green peas (matar) and paneer (Indian cottage cheese), it's a popular dish from North Indian cuisine.

Details

Author: Dassana Amit

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 45 minutes

Cuisine: Indian

Course: Main Dish

Servings: 4

Calories: Approx. 250 kcal per serving

Ingredients

For Masala Paste

  • ½ cup chopped onions
  • ¾ to 1 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon chopped ginger (or ¼ teaspoon ground ginger)
  • 1 teaspoon chopped garlic
  • 10 to 12 cashews
  • 2 tablespoons coriander leaves (cilantro)
  • 1 teaspoon chopped green chilies or serrano peppers
  • 4 to 5 black peppercorns
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds (or 1 teaspoon ground coriander)
  • 1 green cardamom (optional)
  • 2 cloves

For Curry

  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
  • ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ½ teaspoon kashmiri red chili powder or sweet paprika
  • ¼ to ½ teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 to 1.25 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (optional)
  • 250 grams paneer (cubed)

Instructions

Make Masala Paste

  1. Add all the masala paste ingredients into a grinder or blender.
  2. Grind to a smooth paste. Add 2 to 3 tablespoons of water, if necessary. Ensure no small bits of cashews remain in the paste.
  3. Set the masala paste aside.

Cook Masala

  1. Heat 3 tablespoons of oil in a 2-litre pressure cooker or pot over medium-low heat.
  2. Add ½ teaspoon cumin seeds and fry until they splutter.
  3. Add the ground masala paste and mix well.
  4. Be cautious of spluttering; cover the pot partly until it stops spluttering.
  5. Sauté for 10 to 12 minutes until the oil separates from the paste and the mixture thickens.

Add Spices

  1. Add the following dry spice powders to the masala paste:
    • ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder
    • ½ teaspoon kashmiri red chili powder or sweet paprika
    • ¼ to ½ teaspoon garam masala powder
  2. Stir well and sauté for 1 minute. If adding cream or milk, add it now.

Cook the Green Peas

  1. Add 1 cup of rinsed green peas to the masala.
  2. Add 1 to 1.25 cups of water and salt, then mix thoroughly.
  3. If using a pot, cover the pan and simmer on medium-low heat until the peas are soft. Check occasionally to prevent burning. Add more water if necessary.

Pressure Cook (if using a pressure cooker)

  1. Cover the pressure cooker and cook on medium heat for 9 to 10 minutes (2 to 3 whistles).
  2. Once the pressure drops, open the cooker. If the gravy is thin, simmer to thicken.
  3. If you prefer a slightly sweet taste, you can add up to ½ teaspoon sugar.

Add Paneer

  1. Add cubed paneer to the cooked peas and mix gently.
  2. Simmer on low heat for a few minutes until the paneer is heated through.
  3. If desired, add ½ teaspoon crushed kasuri methi (optional) for extra flavor.
  4. Avoid overcooking the paneer to prevent it from becoming rubbery. You can also fry the paneer separately before adding it to the curry.

Final Steps

  1. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with your choice of bread or rice.

Serving Suggestions

Matar Paneer pairs well with Indian flatbreads like Roti, Paratha, or Naan. It also goes wonderfully with Jeera Rice or steamed Basmati Rice. A side vegetable salad or Raita complements this dish perfectly. You can also enjoy it with Poori or regular bread.

Storage and Leftovers

Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 1 to 2 days. The curry will thicken as it cools, so add water while reheating to adjust the consistency.
