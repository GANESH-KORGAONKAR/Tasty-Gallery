Mutton Biryani Recipe

Description

Mutton Biryani is a royal dish with beautifully spiced and fragrant layers of biryani rice centered with juicy, tender mutton (goat meat or lamb) and spices.

Details

Author: Farrukh Aziz

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 1 hour 15 minutes

Marination Time: 2 hours

Total Time: 3 hours 30 minutes

Cuisine: Indian

Course: Main Course

Diet: Gluten-Free, Non-Vegetarian

Servings: 10

Calories: 836 kcal

Ingredients

To grind to a paste (for marination)

  • ¼ cup Coriander Leaves (tightly packed)
  • 4-5 Green Chilies
  • 16–18 Garlic Cloves
  • 3 inches Ginger Piece
  • 1 teaspoon Peppercorns
  • 5-6 Cardamom
  • 6–8 Cloves

To marinate the mutton

  • 1 cup Thick Yoghurt
  • ¼ cup Golden Fried Onions (birista)
  • ½ tablespoon Garam Masala Powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 kilogram Lamb or Goat Meat on bone (cut into medium-sized pieces)

For biryani gravy

  • 8 tablespoons Vegetable Oil (divided)
  • 5 Potatoes (medium, cut in large cubes)
  • 2 Onions (large, finely sliced)
  • 2 inch Cinnamon Stick
  • 2 teaspoons Cumin Seeds
  • 1 teaspoon Turmeric Powder
  • 3-4 teaspoons Kashmiri Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tablespoon Coriander Powder
  • Water as needed
  • 2 Tomatoes (large, sliced)
  • 4-5 Green Chilies (slit or sliced)

To cook the rice

  • 4 cups Long Grain Basmati Rice
  • Water as needed
  • 2–3 Bay Leaves
  • 1 ½ inch Cinnamon Sticks
  • 1 teaspoon Caraway Seeds (shajeera) or Cumin Seeds
  • 3–4 Cloves
  • 5–6 Green Cardamom
  • 1 Star Anise
  • 1 tablespoon Ghee

For layering the biryani

  • ¼ cup Mint leaves
  • 3 tablespoons Ghee
  • A generous pinch of saffron soaked in 4 tablespoons of milk
  • ⅓ cup Caramelized Onions or Fried Onions (Birista)

Instructions

Preparation and Marination

  1. Grind all the ingredients listed under "masalas to grind to paste" into a fine paste.
  2. Mix the ground masala paste, ¼ cup birista, whisked yogurt, garam masala, and salt. Add mutton, mix well, and marinate for at least 2 hours (preferably overnight).
  3. Wash the biryani rice and soak for 45 minutes. Strain and set aside.

Making the Biryani Gravy

  1. Heat 3 tablespoons of oil in a pan and fry potato cubes until golden. Set aside.
  2. Heat remaining oil in a pot, fry onions until soft, add cinnamon sticks and cumin seeds, and cook until golden.
  3. Add marinated mutton, sauté on high for 5-6 minutes, then add turmeric, chili, and coriander powder.
  4. Add 2 cups of water, cover, and cook until the mutton is tender. Add tomatoes, fried potatoes, and chilies, and cook for another 6-8 minutes.

Cooking the Biryani Rice

  1. In a large pot, boil water with bay leaves, cinnamon sticks, shajeera, cloves, cardamom, star anise, ghee, and salt.
  2. Add the soaked rice to the boiling water and cook until 90% done. Strain and set aside.

Layering and Slow Cooking (Dum)

  1. In a large pot, layer half the cooked rice, followed by the mutton masala, mint leaves, ghee, half the saffron milk, and birista. Add the remaining rice on top.
  2. Top with ghee, saffron milk, and reserved oil from the mutton masala.
  3. Cover with a cotton cloth, place the lid with a heavy weight on top, and cook on high for 5 minutes, then on low for 15 minutes. Let rest for 15 minutes.

Serving Suggestions

Serve hot with raita, salad, or boiled eggs.
