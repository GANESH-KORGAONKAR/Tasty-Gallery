Paneer Tikka Recipe

Description

This Restaurant Style Paneer Tikka is a popular and delicious tandoori starter and snack where paneer (Indian cottage cheese cubes) are marinated in a spiced yogurt-based marinade, arranged on skewers and grilled in the oven.

Details

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Marinating Time: 2 hours

Total Time: 2 hours 30 minutes

Cuisine:Indian

Course: Snacks, Starters

Diet: Gluten Free, Vegetarian

Calories: 316

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 200 to 250 grams Paneer (Indian cottage cheese)
  • ½ cup diced onions or 1 medium onion
  • ½ diced capsicum or 1 small to medium bell pepper (green, yellow, or red)

For Paneer Tikka Marination:

  • 200 grams Hung Curd (Greek yogurt)
  • 1 tablespoon Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 2 teaspoons Kashmiri red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur)
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper powder (optional)
  • 1.5 teaspoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon mustard oil or any neutral oil
  • ½ teaspoon black salt (optional)
  • Salt as required
  • Mustard oil for brushing

Instructions

Preparation:

  1. Chop the paneer into square-shaped cubes.
  2. Dice onions and capsicum into 1 to 1.5-inch square pieces.

Making Marination:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk hung curd until smooth.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste, spice powders, carom seeds, black salt, regular salt, lemon juice, and mustard oil.
  3. Mix well and adjust seasonings if needed.
  4. Add veggies and paneer to the marinade. Mix gently to coat evenly.
  5. Cover and marinate for at least 2 hours in the refrigerator.
  6. After marinating, thread paneer and veggies alternately onto bamboo skewers (soak or rinse skewers before use).

Grilling Paneer Tikka:

  1. Preheat oven to 230–240°C (464°F) for 15–20 minutes. Use only the top heating element.
  2. Line a baking tray with foil or parchment paper and arrange the skewers on the tray.
  3. Brush oil on the paneer and veggies.
  4. Grill for 7–10 minutes, then turn skewers and grill for another 3–5 minutes until edges are slightly charred.
  5. Remove from oven, place on a serving plate, sprinkle with chaat masala and lemon juice.

Serving: Serve hot with mint chutney, onion rings, and lemon wedges.

Notes

  • You can use a barbecue grill for cooking.
  • For a richer flavor, marinate the paneer overnight in the refrigerator.
  • Add other vegetables like mushrooms, baby corn, or broccoli for variety.
  • Brush oil to prevent paneer and veggies from drying out during grilling.
  • Vegan options: Replace paneer with tofu or tempeh and use vegan yogurt.
