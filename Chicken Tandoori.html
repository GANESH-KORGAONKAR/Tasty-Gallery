Tandoori Chicken Recipe

Description

Tandoori chicken can be great. Don’t let mediocre restaurant chicken put you off something wonderful. You can make it way better than they can!

Details

Author: romain | glebekitchen

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Marinating Time: Not specified

Cook Time: 40 minutes

Total Time: 55 minutes

Cuisine: Indian

Course: Appetizer or Starter

Servings: 4

Calories: 620 kcal

Ingredients

Tandoori Chicken

  • 1 3-4 lb chicken or a mix of chicken parts
  • Tandoori marinade (recipe below)
  • 1/2 lemon juice
  • 1-2 tbsp ghee to brush (optional)

Tandoori Marinade

  • 1 Tbsp cumin powder
  • 1 Tbsp coriander powder
  • 2 tsp hot curry powder (Madras curry powder)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
  • 1 Tbsp kasoor methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp mint sauce (e.g., Coleman's mint sauce)
  • 1 Tbsp cilantro leaves/stems
  • 1 Tbsp Patak’s tandoori paste (optional)
  • 1 Tbsp garlic ginger paste
  • 3 Tbsp neutral oil (e.g., vegetable or canola)
  • 4-6 Tbsp water (to make a runny paste)
  • 1 pinch red or orange food coloring (optional)

Instructions

  1. Combine all the ingredients for the tandoori marinade except the lemon juice. Add the lemon juice just before cooking.
  2. Cut the chicken into 10 pieces, removing skin where specified. Leave skin on the wings.
  3. Combine the chicken with the marinade and refrigerate for 1–12 hours.
  4. Preheat the grill for high indirect heat (above 500°F if possible).
  5. Grill the chicken over indirect heat for 20–25 minutes or until internal temperature reaches 155°F.
  6. Move the chicken to direct heat and grill for 2–4 minutes, turning frequently to char lightly.
  7. Brush with ghee (if using) and serve with cilantro, thinly sliced onion or shallot, and raita.

Notes

  • For homemade garlic ginger paste, it’s better to make fresh.
  • Tandoori Chicken in the Oven: Add extra oil to the marinade. Preheat to 450°F and use a hot baking dish for roasting. Flip the chicken during cooking and finish with broiler char.
