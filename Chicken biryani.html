Chicken Biryani Recipe

Description

Authentic Chicken Biryani with tender pieces of chicken marinated in warming spices, layered with aromatic long-grain Basmati rice, topped with caramelized onions and saffron milk.

Details

Author: Archana Mundhe

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Marinating Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Cuisine: Indian

Course: Main Course

Servings: 6

Calories: 601 kcal

Equipment

  • 4-QT Casserole
  • 3-QT Saucepan

Ingredients

Marinade Ingredients

  • 1 pound chicken drumsticks (4), skin removed
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut in half
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon Kashmiri red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ¼ cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • ¾ cup plain yogurt

Crispy Fried Onions

  • 4 tablespoons ghee
  • 1 large yellow onion, thinly sliced

Rice Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 2½ cups extra long grain Basmati rice
  • 1 teaspoon black cumin seeds (shah jeera)
  • 2 bay leaves (tamal patra)
  • 1 star anise
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • 8 cloves
  • 1½ tablespoons kosher salt

Garnish

  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro
  • ½ teaspoon saffron
  • 2 tablespoons warm milk

Instructions

Marinate Chicken

  1. Make the marinade by mixing yogurt, ginger, garlic, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, salt, mint leaves, and lemon juice.
  2. Add chicken and coat evenly with the marinade. Keep in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes or overnight for best results.
  3. Soak saffron in warm milk and reserve.

Rinse & Soak Rice

  1. Rinse and drain the Basmati rice 2 to 3 times.
  2. Add 4 cups of water and let it soak for 20 minutes.

Fry Onions

  1. While the rice is soaking, add ghee to a heavy-bottomed pan and fry onions on medium heat until light golden brown (about 15–20 minutes).
  2. Remove the fried onions, leaving the excess ghee behind for cooking the chicken.
  3. Add one-fourth of the fried onions to the marinated chicken and mix.

Parboil Rice

  1. In a medium pot, boil 8 cups of water with cumin seeds, bay leaves, star anise, cardamom, cloves, and salt.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and add to the boiling water. Cook until about 90% done (5–6 minutes).
  3. Drain promptly and reserve.

Cook Chicken

  1. In the same pan with leftover ghee, add marinated chicken and cook on medium heat for 8–10 minutes, turning halfway.

Layer and Cook Biryani on Dum

  1. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken.
  2. Top with caramelized onions and saffron-infused milk.
  3. Cover the pot and seal with aluminum foil. Place weight on the lid and cook on low heat for 20 minutes.
  4. Turn off heat and let it sit for 10 minutes before opening the pot.
  5. Garnish with cilantro, fluff the rice gently, and serve.

Notes

  • Chicken can be marinated for up to 24 hours for enhanced flavor.
  • Fry onions a day ahead to save time.
  • Use aged, extra-long grain Basmati rice for authentic flavor.
