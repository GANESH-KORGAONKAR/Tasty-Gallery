Gulab Jamun with Khoya

Description

Gulab Jamun are soft, delicious, berry-sized balls made from milk solids, flour, and a leavening agent. These golden balls are soaked in a fragrant rose-flavored sugar syrup, making them irresistible!

Details

Author: Swasthi's Recipes

Prep Time: 25 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Total Time: 40 minutes

Cuisine: Indian

Course: Dessert

Servings: 14-16 balls

Calories: Approx. 120 kcal per ball

Ingredients

For Gulab Jamun

  • 125 grams khoya or mawa (evaporated milk solids) (1 heaped cup, grated)
  • 2½ tablespoons Maida (all-purpose flour)
  • ¼ teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 to 4 tablespoons milk or water (use more if needed)
  • 2 tablespoons paneer (optional)
  • Oil or ghee as needed for frying

For Sugar Syrup

  • 1 ¼ to 1 ½ cups sugar
  • ¾ cup water
  • ½ teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)
  • 1½ teaspoon lemon juice

Instructions

Make Sugar Syrup

  1. To a wide pot, add sugar and water. Heat on a medium flame until the sugar dissolves, then let the syrup boil until it reaches a sticky consistency.
  2. Take ¼ tsp of it, cool slightly, and check by separating your thumb and forefinger—it should be sticky.
  3. Add rose water, cardamom powder, and lemon juice. Mix well and set aside to keep the syrup slightly hot.

Making Dough

  1. In a mixing bowl, stir together grated mawa, flour, cardamom powder, and baking powder.
  2. Pour milk little by little, kneading gently to form a smooth, crack-free dough. Avoid overkneading to maintain the texture.
  3. Divide the dough into 14-16 parts.
  4. Take each ball in your palms, rub it gently a few times until smooth, then roll it into a crack-free ball.

Frying Gulab Jamun

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a deep pot or kadai. Check the oil temperature by gently sliding a small piece of dough—it should rise slowly with bubbles, not brown.
  2. Regulate the flame to medium. Drop one ball at a time, adding as many as can fit comfortably in the pot.
  3. Fry the balls, stirring occasionally, until golden brown. Adjust the flame to medium-low if needed to fry them uniformly.
  4. Remove the fried gulab jamuns to a colander, and continue frying the rest.

Soaking the Gulab Jamun

  1. Allow the gulab jamuns to cool for 3-5 minutes before adding them to the slightly hot sugar syrup.
  2. Gently rotate the gulab jamuns in the syrup, ensuring they soak for 45 minutes before serving.
  3. Serve gulab jamun with sugar syrup or vanilla ice cream, garnished with thinly sliced pistachios (optional).

Don’ts for the Sugar Syrup

  • Avoid using a cast iron pot for the syrup, as it can cause crystallization of the syrup after cooling down.
